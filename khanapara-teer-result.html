📜 Khanapara Teer History, Tradition & Culture

  • 🏹 Origin & Development

    Khanapara Teer originated as a regional variant of the traditional Shillong Teer game, played in the Khanapara area of Assam, near the Meghalaya border. It shares the same archery-based lottery system but has its own unique cultural identity and local flavor.

  • 🎯 Local Traditions

    The Khanapara Teer grounds have become a hub for local communities, where players gather daily to witness the matches. The game has become an integral part of the local economy and social life, with many families having generations of archers.

  • 🏛️ Modern Impact

    Khanapara Teer has grown significantly in popularity, attracting players from Assam and neighboring states. It operates under the same legal framework as Shillong Teer and follows similar rules and timings, with FR at 4:10 PM and SR at 5:05 PM.

  • 📌 Did You Know?

    Khanapara Teer has become a major tourist attraction, with visitors coming from across Assam to witness the unique archery competitions and the vibrant atmosphere of the grounds.
