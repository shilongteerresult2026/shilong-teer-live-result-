📜 Shillong Teer History, Tradition & Culture

  • 🏹 Origin & Cultural Significance

    Shillong Teer is not merely a numbers game; it is a living cultural heritage of the Khasi community in Meghalaya. Originating during the British colonial era (1830s-1940s), archery was traditionally a means of self-defense and a royal sport among the indigenous Khasi tribes. The game transformed into a unique lottery system in the early 20th century. In 1982, the Meghalaya government legally recognized Teer under the Meghalaya Amusement and Betting Tax Act, making it one of the few legally sanctioned betting games in India.

  • 🎯 Traditional Rituals

    Before each match, local elders and priests perform traditional ceremonies to bless the archers and the counting process. These rituals ensure fair play and spiritual protection. The game is deeply rooted in Khasi culture, with archery being a symbol of strength, precision, and community bonding.

  • 🏛️ Modern Evolution

    Today, over 200 licensed Teer counters operate across Meghalaya, processing millions of rupees in bets daily. The game has survived through generations, adapting to modern times while preserving its traditional essence. It stands as a unique blend of ancient archery skills and modern lottery systems, attracting players from all walks of life.

  • 📌 Did You Know?

    The oldest recorded Teer match dates back to 1906 when local Khasi kings organized archery competitions during harvest festivals. The game was played with traditional bamboo bows and wooden arrows.
