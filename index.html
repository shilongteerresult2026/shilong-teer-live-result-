📌 Historical Background: Shillong Teer originated during the British colonial era (1830s-1940s) in the Khasi Hills of Meghalaya. Archery was traditionally a means of self-defense and a royal sport among the indigenous Khasi tribes. The game transformed into a unique lottery system in the early 20th century. In 1982, the Meghalaya government legally recognized Teer under the Meghalaya Amusement and Betting Tax Act, making it one of the few legally sanctioned betting games in India.

📍 Where is it played? The game is held Monday to Friday at the famous Polo Bazar and Rilbong grounds in Shillong, attracting thousands of spectators daily. The oldest recorded Teer match dates back to 1906 when local Khasi kings organized archery competitions during harvest festivals. Today, over 200 licensed Teer counters operate across Meghalaya, processing millions of rupees in bets daily.

🏆 Cultural Impact: Teer is not just a game but a socio-cultural event in Meghalaya. The game employs hundreds of archers, counter staff, and supports the local economy.   • ⏰ First Round (FR): 4:00 PM IST | Second Round (SR): 5:00 PM IST.
