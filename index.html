📜 TRADITIONAL HISTORY & GAME DETAILS

🏹 Shillong Teer — Meghalaya's Century-Old Tradition▼
Origin & History: During the British era, archery in the Khasi hills was a means of self-defense. In the 1980s, Shillong Teer received legal recognition. Now it's a cultural symbol held Mon-Fri at Politan Bazar.
🎯 Game Rules:
  • 50 archers, each 30 arrows.
  • Last two digits of total arrows = winning number.
  • FR 4:00 PM, SR 5:00 PM.
  • House & Ending numbers from local calculations.
🏹 Khanapara Teer — Assam-Meghalaya Border▼
Started in 1990s in Assam, approved by govt. Now equally popular. First round result 4:00 PM, second 5:00 PM.
🏹 Juwai Teer — Jewel of Jaintia Hills▼
Began early 2000s, traditional drumming before rounds. FR 2:00 PM, SR 3:30 PM approx.
