🏹 Shillong Teer — Complete History & Origin | শিলং তীর — সম্পূর্ণ ইতিহাস ও উৎপত্তি

📌 Origin and Historical Background / উৎপত্তি ও ঐতিহাসিক পটভূমি: Shillong Teer originated during the British colonial era (1830s-1940s) in the Khasi Hills of Meghalaya. Archery was traditionally a means of self-defense and a royal sport among the indigenous Khasi tribes. The game transformed into a unique lottery system in the early 20th century. In 1982, the Meghalaya government legally recognized Teer under the Meghalaya Amusement and Betting Tax Act, making it one of the few legally sanctioned betting games in India.

📍 Where is it played? / কোথায় খেলা হয়? The game is held Monday to Friday at the famous Polo Bazar and Rilbong grounds in Shillong, attracting thousands of spectators daily. The oldest recorded Teer match dates back to 1906 when local Khasi kings organized archery competitions during harvest festivals. Today, over 200 licensed Teer counters operate across Meghalaya, processing millions of rupees in bets daily.

📋 Complete Game Rules & Mechanism / সম্পূর্ণ খেলার নিয়ম ও প্রক্রিয়া:
  • 🏹 50 expert archers participate in each round, each shooting 30 arrows.
  • 📊 Total arrows shot range between 1200 to 1500 per round.
  • 🎯 Winning number = LAST TWO DIGITS of total arrows that hit the target.
  • ⏰ First Round (FR): 4:00 PM | Second Round (SR): 5:00 PM (IST).
  • 📢 Results are announced publicly at the counter immediately after counting.
  • ✋ Each arrow is counted by hand to ensure transparency and fairness.
  • 📅 The game runs Monday to Friday; Saturday and Sunday are weekly off days.
  • 💰 Minimum bet amount is ₹1 and maximum can go up to ₹10,000 per counter.

🏆 Cultural Impact & Records / সাংস্কৃতিক প্রভাব ও রেকর্ড: Teer is not just a game but a socio-cultural event in Meghalaya. The game employs hundreds of archers, counter staff, and supports the local economy. Traditional Khasi chants and rituals are performed before each match. The highest recorded arrow hits in a single round was 1478 in 2019, while the lowest was 892 due to heavy rainfall. More than 15,000 people visit Shillong Teer counters daily. The annual revenue from Teer in Meghalaya exceeds ₹100 crores. The oldest Teer counter in Shillong has been operating continuously for over 65 years since 1961.
