📜 TRADITIONAL HISTORY & GAME DETAILS

🏹 Shillong Teer — Meghalaya's Century-Old Tradition

Origin and Historical Significance: Shillong Teer originated during the British colonial era in the Khasi Hills of Meghalaya. Archery was traditionally a means of self-defense and a royal sport among the indigenous Khasi tribes. The game transformed into a unique lottery system in the early 20th century. In 1982, the Meghalaya government legally recognized Teer under the Meghalaya Amusement and Betting Tax Act, making it one of the few legally sanctioned betting games in India. The game is held Monday to Friday at the famous Polo Bazar and Rilbong grounds, attracting thousands of spectators daily.

Complete Game Rules and Mechanism:
  • 50 expert archers participate in each round, each shooting 30 arrows.
  • Total arrows shot range between 1200 to 1500 per round.
  • Winning number = LAST TWO DIGITS of total arrows that hit the target.
  • First Round (FR): 4:00 PM | Second Round (SR): 5:00 PM (IST).
  • Results are announced publicly at the counter immediately after counting.
  • Each arrow is counted by hand to ensure transparency and fairness.
  • The game runs Monday to Friday; Saturday and Sunday are weekly off days.

Cultural Impact and Records: Teer is not just a game but a socio-cultural event in Meghalaya. The game employs hundreds of archers, counter staff, and supports the local economy. Traditional Khasi chants and rituals are performed before each match. The highest recorded arrow hits in a single round was 1478 in 2019, while the lowest was 892 due to heavy rainfall.

Interesting Facts: The word "Teer" means arrow in the Khasi language. The game has been featured in several documentaries about Northeast Indian culture. Many local families have been involved in Teer for generations, passing down their archery skills and prediction techniques. The oldest Teer counter in Shillong has been operating for over 60 years. Each day, approximately 2,000 to 3,000 people visit the Shillong Teer counter to place their bets.

🏹 Khanapara Teer — Assam-Meghalaya Border Legacy

Location and History: Khanapara Teer is played at the Assam-Meghalaya border near Guwahati. It started in 1995 after receiving official approval from the Assam government. This venue has gained massive popularity due to its high participation and reliable result declaration system. The Khanapara Teer counter is one of the busiest in Northeast India, processing thousands of bets daily.

Timings and Process: First Round 4:00 PM, Second Round 5:00 PM (IST). The calculation method remains identical to Shillong Teer: total arrows hitting the target determine the winning numbers. Many professional bettors consider Khanapara results as a crucial indicator for next-day predictions.

Unique Features: Khanapara often witnesses higher arrow counts because of larger archer teams (sometimes up to 60 archers). The venue is known for its transparent counting process, where each arrow is counted publicly by three independent counters. Khanapara results are often considered more stable due to consistent weather conditions at the venue.

Betting Trends: Khanapara sees maximum betting on Tuesday and Wednesday. The average number of bets placed daily ranges from 50,000 to 1,00,000. The highest recorded winning payout in Khanapara was ₹25 lakhs in 2018. During peak season, the venue handles over 1.5 lakh bets per day.

🏹 Juwai Teer — Jewel of Jaintia Hills

Cultural Heritage: Juwai Teer began in the early 2000s in the Jaintia Hills district, near the Bangladesh border. What makes Juwai unique is the traditional drumming ceremony performed before each round, invoking local deities and blessings. The game is deeply rooted in community bonding and indigenous faith.

Timings: First Round at 2:00 PM, Second Round at 3:30 PM (IST) – earlier than other venues, making it the first result of the day. Many players track Juwai results as morning indicators for Shillong evening games.

Local Traditions: Before each Teer round, a local priest performs a small ritual with rice and flowers to ward off evil spirits. The archers wear traditional Jaintia attire during special occasions. Juwai results are announced with a ceremonial gong sound, a practice unique to this venue. The arrow counting is done on a traditional wooden platform, adding to its rustic charm.

Special Characteristics: Juwai Teer is known for its unpredictable results, making it exciting for experienced players. The venue is smaller than Shillong and Khanapara, creating an intimate atmosphere for bettors. The local community strongly participates in this venue, making it a true community event.
